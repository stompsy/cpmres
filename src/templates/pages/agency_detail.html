The origin
Jan, 2019
Community Paramedic Program pilot begins
In late 2018, Port Angeles Fire Department (PAFD) took a “leap of faith” and moved forward with implementing a pilot Community Paramedicine program. The intent of the pilot program was to proactively address gaps in the health & social services system by utilizing an existing Firefighter/Paramedic FTE from within PAFD for a one-year period beginning January 2019.

Jan, 2021
Hargrove Grant funding
In the 2005 Washington State legislative session, Senator Hargrove passed the Omnibus Mental Health and Substance Abuse Reform Act SB 563 to expand substance abuse and mental health treatment. The bill allows local governments to increase a sales tax to improve local services. In March 2006, the Clallam County Board of Commissioners (BOCC) approved an ordinance authorizing a 1/10th of one percent sales and use tax. This sales and use tax is known as the Behavioral Health Tax or BH Tax.
Grant funding
Feb, 2021
PAFD & North Olympic Healthcare Network partner
The Port Angeles Community Paramedicine program received funding to provide medical and social services through 2023. A memorandum of agreement with North Olympic Healthcare Network (NOHN) was signed allowing for extension of the pilot program that already provides a range of services to heavy users of the 9-1-1 system. NOHN will use grant funding to provide up to $220,000 annually to support two full-time community paramedics through the end of 2023, according to the proposal.
